What Makes Taylor Swift's Music so Successful?

A dive into the singer's music
Iris Araki

Background

Taylor Swift has been in the public eye and media ever since she was 16 - and even years before then. Swift is a generational talent, with little to no argument. Though many people have mixed opinions about her, her individual impact on the music industry is clear and significant, making her one of the most notable figures in the industry itself. Releasing over 24 albums and EPs, Swift’s analytical statistics of many categories, spanning from danceability to popularity, are useful to decipher what fans of Swift favor. With the new emergence of pop-talent, such as Sabrina Carpenter and Olivia Rodrigo, analyzing Swift’s success through data can offer strategies for emerging artists to replicate to further launch their careers.

Summary

This data memo summarizes findings regarding singer and songwriter Taylor Swift, collected by user Jarred Priester. This data analyzes Swift’s discography and data found on several factors, such as danceability and popularity. This dataset is hand collected and factors, such as danceability and popularity, are calculated on a numerical scale The reliability and validity of the dataset was determined by a random selection of 10 rows and through fact-checking. Though Priester includes many of Swift’s songs, I focused on songs that are found in both the primary and secondary dataset.

Findings

  • Each song's page views on lyric website genuis.com are not accurately related to a song’s popularity, but are somewhat similar. About 13% of the data shows that the popularity shown in the primary dataset accurately reflects genius.com clicks in the second dataset.
  • The average score for danceability decreases as Taylor Swift releases more albums. Swift's highest danceability average was for Lover and reputation (0.66), whereas her lowest was for evermore (deluxe version) and The Tortured Poet's Department: The Anthology (0.53). Ever since Lover, Swift has made albums that are averaging 0.63 or less on danceability.
  • A majority - 60% - of Taylor Swift’s songs are written by Jack Antonoff.
